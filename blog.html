How to Calculate Zakat Correctly

Published: June 1, 2026

Zakat is one of the Five Pillars of Islam. It is an obligatory form of almsgiving calculated as 2.5% of your total wealth. Understanding the correct calculation is important for fulfilling this religious obligation.

To calculate Zakat correctly, you must:

  • Gather all assets (cash, savings, investments, property)
  • Subtract debts and liabilities
  • Calculate 2.5% of the net amount

Understanding Fitrana - Zakat al-Fitr

Published: June 1, 2026

Fitrana, also known as Zakat al-Fitr, is a form of charity given during Ramadan before the Eid prayers. It purifies the fasting person from any indecency or improper act that may have occurred during the month.

Key points about Fitrana:

  • It is obligatory for all Muslims
  • Standard amount is 1.6 kg of wheat or rice per person
  • Can be given in cash equivalent
  • Must be given before Eid prayers

The Importance of Namaz in Daily Life

Published: June 1, 2026

Salah (prayer) is the second pillar of Islam and one of the most important practices. Performing Namaz five times daily is mandatory for all adult Muslims and brings numerous spiritual and physical benefits.

Benefits of regular prayer:

  • Strengthens faith and connection with Allah
  • Provides mental peace and reduces anxiety
  • Creates discipline and structure in life
  • Unites the Muslim community

The Islamic Hijri Calendar Explained

Published: June 1, 2026

The Hijri calendar is the lunar calendar used in the Islamic world. It consists of 12 months with 354 or 355 days, making it about 11 days shorter than the Gregorian year.

Important information:

  • Year 1 AH corresponds to 622 CE (Gregorian)
  • Months are determined by moon sightings
  • Islamic holidays follow the Hijri calendar
  • Ramadan is the 9th month

Duas - Islamic Prayers for Daily Life

Published: June 1, 2026

Making Dua (supplication) is a form of worship and communication with Allah. There are specific duas for various situations in our daily lives - for seeking provision, protection during travel, overcoming difficulties, and more.

Why duas are important:

  • Direct connection with Allah
  • Shows humility and dependence on God
  • Brings peace and comfort
  • Can be made anytime, anywhere
